Cricket is a bat-and-ball game played between two teams of eleven players on a field,
at the centre of which is a 22-yard (20-metre) pitch with a wicket at each end.
Each wicket consists of two bails balanced on three stumps.

The game has two teams that take turns batting and fielding.
The batting team scores runs by hitting the ball and running between the wickets,
while the fielding team tries to dismiss the batters.

Cricket is particularly popular in South Asia, Australasia, the United Kingdom,
southern Africa, and the West Indies. The International Cricket Council (ICC)
governs international cricket.

A match has two innings per side in Test cricket, while One Day Internationals
and Twenty20 matches have one innings each. Cricket originated in England
and is now played and followed worldwide.
